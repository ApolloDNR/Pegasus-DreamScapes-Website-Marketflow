Yes. I looked through the current direction, the Claude log, and the operating manual. The big answer is this:

**You should not redesign the website anymore. You should keep the Replit visual direction and rebuild the copy/structure/funnels inside that style.**

The manual makes the website direction much clearer: Pegasus is not “we buy houses cash,” not just a flip company, not just a brokerage, and not just software. It is a **strategy-first real estate operating company** that turns property situations into structured outcomes through intake, underwriting, deal structuring, and execution. 

## My judgment on the website direction

What you like is probably the right instinct:

```txt
Keep:
- The colors
- The front/hero visual feel
- The warm real estate look
- The featured/case study/project style
- The approachable public-facing vibe
```

What needs to change is not the “skin.” It is the **message, structure, and funnels.**

Right now the website needs to be shaped around the actual Pegasus operating model:

```txt
1. Property owners submit a situation.
2. Pegasus reviews the situation.
3. Peggy / intake collects the right info.
4. Pegasus routes the lead into the right lane.
5. The lane becomes acquisition, wholesale, JV, KW listing, referral, MarketFlow/private distribution, incubation, or archive intelligence.
```

That “No Lead Dies” doctrine from the manual is the website funnel. The website should not just be “sell your house” or “invest with us.” It should say:

> Every property situation deserves a strategy review. If there is a path, Pegasus helps identify it. If there is not, Pegasus tells the truth and routes the opportunity to its next best lawful path.

That is strong. That is different. That is your real hook.

**Headline:**

**Copy:**

